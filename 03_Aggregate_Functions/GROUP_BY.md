# GROUP BY Clause

## Overview

The GROUP BY clause groups rows that have the same values into summary rows.

It is often used with aggregate functions such as COUNT(), SUM(), AVG(), MIN(), and MAX().

## Why It Matters

Data Analysts use GROUP BY to:

- Calculate revenue by product
- Count customers by city
- Analyze sales by category
- Create business reports

---

## Sample Dataset

### Orders Table

| Order_ID | City | Revenue |
|----------|------|----------|
| 101 | Mumbai | 500 |
| 102 | Delhi | 1000 |
| 103 | Mumbai | 700 |
| 104 | Delhi | 800 |

---

## Syntax

```sql
SELECT column_name,
       aggregate_function(column_name)
FROM table_name
GROUP BY column_name;
```

---

## Example 1

Count customers in each city.

```sql
SELECT City,
       COUNT(*)
FROM Orders
GROUP BY City;
```

### Result

| City | COUNT(*) |
|------|-----------|
| Mumbai | 2 |
| Delhi | 2 |

---

## Example 2

Calculate total revenue by city.

```sql
SELECT City,
       SUM(Revenue)
FROM Orders
GROUP BY City;
```

### Result

| City | SUM(Revenue) |
|------|-------------|
| Mumbai | 1200 |
| Delhi | 1800 |

---

## Visual Explanation

Before GROUP BY

| City | Revenue |
|------|---------|
| Mumbai | 500 |

| Delhi | 800 |

After GROUP BY

| City | Total Revenue |
|------|---------------|
| Mumbai | 1200 |

---

## Business Use Case

Find revenue generated by each city.

```sql
SELECT City,
       SUM(Revenue) AS Total_Revenue
FROM Orders
GROUP BY City;
```

This helps businesses identify high-performing regions.

---

## Common Mistakes

### Missing GROUP BY Column

Incorrect:

```sql
SELECT City,
       SUM(Revenue)
FROM Orders;
```

Correct:

```sql
SELECT City,
       SUM(Revenue)
FROM Orders
GROUP BY City;
```
